Medical Negligence
AdminMay 5, 2020

What is Medical Negligence?

  • A doctor owes certain duties toward his patient who approaches him for medical care. A deficiency in his duties give rise to action for damages.
  • Negligence is error on the part of doctor, including error in diagnosis, medication dosage, health management, treatment or aftercare.

Types of Medical Negligence

  • Unhygienic surroundings of  hospital
  • Leaving instrument inside the body
  • Amputation of wrong limb
  • Wrong medicine
  • Over dosing of medicine
  • Not matching the adequate standard of profession

Requisites of Negligence

  • Legal duty to exercise due care
  • Breach of said duty
  • Injury caused due to lack of such care
  • Entitlement of Consequential damages

REMEDIES

  • CIVIL
    Relief is given in compensation under the Consumer Protection Act ,1985.
    A disciplinary proceedings can be initiated against the doctor by filing the complaint before the medical council of India. It can only warn the doctor, suspend or revoke the license.
  • CRIMINAL
    A criminal case can be filed under section 304A or 336, 337, 338 under which a doctor can be imprisoned for 2 to 3 yrs or with fine or with both.
